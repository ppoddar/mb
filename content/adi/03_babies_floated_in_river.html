One day, young Shantanu was roaming in the banks of river Ganga. He noticed an exquisite woman in a golden dress. Shantanu was enchanted by her.

Shantanu approached her: "Oh my beauty, who are you? A goddess, or a mere mortal? Whoever you are, will you please marry me?".

The lady replied:"I am Ganga. I can be your wife. But on one condition. You must not resist any of my actions, be it good or evil. If you ever object, I will leave you ".

Shantanu, enchanted by the mysterious woman, agreed.

Shantanu was happy with his charming and caring new wife. They together had eight babies, all boys, all handsome as angels.

But every time, his wife threw the newborn into the river just after birth. Shantanu was irked by her strange act, but he did not resist.

Nevertheless, he could not help himself for the eighth newborn."Please do not throw the baby in the water": he pleaded to her. "Why do you reject the babies every time. Why?"

His wife smiled: "You want a son, I know. I will not throw this newborn away to the river. But my days with you have now come to an end".

Then she revealed her identity, told him about the curse of OctoBasus...

Once OctoBasus and their wives went on a vacation to Sumeru mountain near Saint Vashistha's meditation retreat.
The wife of DuyBasu - one of the OctoBasus - saw Nandini - the amazing cow that granted every wish of its owner - grazing on the campus.
DuyBasu's wife liked the cow so much that she must gift it to her friend Jitabati.
She insisted husband so much that finally DuyBasu had to steal the cow.
Saint Vashishta was not present at the campus at that time. But once he returned and found theÂ cow missing, he was furious.
He cursed: "Whoever has stolen my Nandini will be born as human".
OctoBasu apologized and begged Saint Vashishta to retract his curse.
Vashishta calmed down a bit: "OK, you will be free of the curse within an year".
"But not him": Vashishta said, pointing at DuyBasu: "He must serve the sentence of a full human life. But... it will be a pious life of a wise man who would be dear to his father and free of sexual temptation".
After narrating the tale of of OctoBasus, Ganga told Shantanu: "O my beloved king, I had thrown the newborns to the river every time because they were OctoBasus reincarnated, and they wished to return to heaven as soon as possible. But this eighth newborn is DuyBasu - he must live a human man life before he can reenter heaven".
Then she took the newborn with her and disappeared into her own river.
